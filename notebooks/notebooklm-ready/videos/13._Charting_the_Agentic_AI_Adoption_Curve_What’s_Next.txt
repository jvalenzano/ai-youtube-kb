## Summary
- EY's technology pulse poll shows 81% of tech executives are highly optimistic about Agentic AI potential, with 43% increasing AI budgets and over half going to autonomous AI
- Agentic AI represents a shift from individual AI assistants to collaborative autonomous agents that can reason, make decisions, and work together in workflows
- Innovation cycles are moving 5-10x faster than cloud migration, requiring companies to experiment quickly or risk falling behind permanently
- Most enterprises are moving away from building custom models to using RAG approaches and Agentic RAG, with focus on orchestrating pre-built agents
- Trust, adaptability, and decision intelligence are becoming critical requirements as agents move from productivity tools to business-critical decision makers
- Data infrastructure remains the foundation - companies must get their data architecture right before deploying effective AI agents
- Talent crisis emerging with 2.3x more demand than supply for AI professionals, plus 90% of companies unsure what AI skills they actually need
- New business models are emerging based on outcome-based pricing rather than traditional seat-based or transaction-based SaaS models
- Security and privacy concerns are the top barrier to adoption, mentioned by over 75% of survey respondents
- Startups have unprecedented advantages in this cycle, able to target vertical labor spend rather than just IT spend with AI-native approaches

## Key Takeaways
- **DO:** Focus on getting your data infrastructure and architecture in place first - there's no AI without good IA (information architecture)
- **DO:** Start experimenting with AI agents now and build flexible frameworks that allow plugging in and pulling out different solutions as the market evolves
- **DO:** Look for AI agent platforms that support open standards like Model Context Protocol (MCP) to avoid vendor lock-in
- **DO:** Target vertical-specific use cases and labor spend rather than broad horizontal IT applications for maximum impact
- **DON'T:** Don't assume you need to build AI models from scratch - focus on RAG approaches and orchestrating pre-built agents instead
- **DON'T:** Don't get overconfident about your competitive position - benchmark against external market developments, not just internal progress
